## Hackathon concept: end-to-end automated sales pipeline

Fully automated sales process from lead to content delivery, with minimal human input.

**Flow:** Pharma browses topic shop → configures offer → pays (PayPal for the demo) → fills out briefing form → Referent builds content → lands on the platform.

- **Pharma dashboard** extended with a shop page showing curated topics.
  - Topics are sourced from the existing Future Plan / topic inbox UI (already partially built): topics get submitted to an inbox, Medical reviews/works through a checklist (target audience, format, conflict-of-topic checks on the platform, etc.), and only approved topics become visible to Sales — and then to Pharma in the shop.
- **Cost configurator** integrated into checkout (password needs recovering before the event).
- **Referent side:** receives the briefing, builds the presentation live in the tool, submits → video gets published.
- **AI agents** monitor progress, send nudges via email, escalate if needed. Optional: an AI PM in ClickUp tracking project status.

## Pitch strategy and presentation format

- Show the journey from each actor's perspective: **Pharma**, **Referent**, **DX employee** (the DX employee just sits there drinking a beer while Slack updates roll in automatically — "new topic submitted", "offer booked", "video viewed" — a running gag).
- Humor angle: Murphy's Law framing — e.g. the referent mistypes an input and the AI gracefully corrects it ("turning shit into gold").
- Persona: young SME (KMU) marketing assistant, ~mid-20s, ~€10k budget authority, wants to self-serve fast without dealing with sales.
- Quantify savings for pitch impact — e.g. "saves 1M minutes/year", "10x project throughput". Any number that makes the audience go "wow, that's the win" is good pitch material.
